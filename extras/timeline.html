Timeline of Cell Classification Schemes

  • 1839
    Schwann proposed a five-group classification for tissue types: isolated/independent cells, independent cells in combination, cells welded by intercellular substance, fibre cells, and cells with broken-down walls (Schwann, 1839; Hall, 1969).
  • 1989
    Junqueira, Carneiro & Kelley proposed a four-group subdivision: epithelial, muscular, nervous, and connective tissues (Junqueira et al., 1989).
  • 1970
    Willmer classified progenitor cells based on morphology and behaviour in culture into four groups: epitheliocytes, mechanocytes, amoebocytes, and nerve cells (Willmer, 1970).
  • 1997
    Stevens & Lowe categorised cell types by function into eight groups: epithelial, support, contractile, nerve, germ, blood, immune, and hormone-secreting cells (Stevens & Lowe, 1997).
  • 1989
    Alberts et al. listed 210 human cell types in 20 categories, mostly related to function (Alberts et al., 1989).
